# BondMap Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 23, 2026

This policy describes BondMap version 2.0.0.

## Contact

Questions about this policy may be sent to support@passmate.jp.

## Accounts and Cloud Sync

BondMap does not require login, create user accounts, or provide cloud sync.

## Location Permission and Use

BondMap requests When In Use location permission. It does not request Always Location access or use background location.

Location is used while BondMap is open to:

- show your current position, location status, update time, and accuracy on the map;
- save a check-in when you explicitly choose to do so;
- save a place from your current location or a map position;
- prepare an Apple Maps link and text when you explicitly choose Share Current Location.

Location sharing is manual. BondMap displays a preview first, and nothing is sent until you tap Share and choose a destination in the iOS Share Sheet. BondMap does not provide live location sharing, automatic Circle sharing, background location sharing, or stealth location features.

The developer does not collect or upload your precise location, check-ins, or saved places to a developer server.

## Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your device:

- check-ins, which may include coordinates, date and time, place name, and an optional note;
- saved places, which may include a name, coordinates, category, color, icon, and optional note;
- Safety Timer state and history;
- trusted contacts you enter manually, which may include a name and optional phone number or email address;
- app preferences, including onboarding and map control state.

BondMap does not read your system Contacts library. Trusted Contacts are for local reference only. BondMap does not automatically contact them.

You can delete check-ins, saved places, or all local BondMap data from the Privacy Center. Deleting the app also removes its local data, subject to normal iOS backup behavior.

## Safety Timer and Notifications

The Safety Timer stores its end time locally and may schedule a local notification to remind you to check the app. The notification is shown to you on your device. BondMap does not automatically contact emergency services or other people.

## Advertising

BondMap uses Google AdMob banner ads only. It does not include interstitial, rewarded, app open, or native ads.

BondMap configures ads for non-personalized treatment, disables Google Mobile Ads publisher first-party ID, and does not request App Tracking Transparency permission or access the IDFA for tracking across other companies' apps or websites.

Google and AdMob may process information for limited or non-personalized ad delivery, frequency capping, fraud prevention, aggregated reporting, and diagnostics. Depending on device settings, region, consent choices, and Google services, this may include IP-derived approximate location, app- or developer-scoped device identifiers, advertising data, ad interaction data, usage data, and diagnostics. See Google's policies for details.

BondMap does not provide AdMob with your precise location, check-ins, saved places, Safety Timer data, or trusted contacts.

## Support Email

Contact Support is optional. If you choose it, BondMap opens your email app with diagnostic text such as app version, build, platform, and OS version. You decide whether to send the email and may edit its content. The developer receives only what you choose to email.

## AI, Purchases, and Subscriptions

BondMap does not include AI features, subscriptions, in-app purchases, or paid ad removal.

## Children's Privacy

BondMap is not designed to collect personal information from children. It has no account, cloud sync, or developer-server location storage. A parent or guardian should supervise a child's use of location features, manual sharing, and device permissions where required.

If you believe a child has provided personal information through an optional support email, contact support@passmate.jp.

## Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when BondMap's features or legal requirements change. The effective date at the top identifies the latest revision.
